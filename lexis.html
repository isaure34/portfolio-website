Overview

Introduction to design thinking. Developed a mobile prototype for a mobile application to learn vocabulary using the prototype building tool Marvel. I then went back 6 months later to create high fidelity wireframes of the prototype on Figma.

Stage 1

Market analysis

what apps already exist and what problem are they trying to solve? Are they being successful? How could my application be different? I looked at Quizlet, StudyBlue, and Answers

Framing the problem

Vocabulary learning apps are often used as a tool to study for exams and are not used outside of that context. My hypothesis was that by adding a game aspect to the application, it will make the process of learning vocabulary more entertaining and less stressful.

Stage 2

User flows

The user flows help address the information architecture of the application and provide context to the features that I wanted to design. I created two user flows: Searching words and practicing words. The challenge is that user flows should be simple and straightforward and it is very possible to think too much about it. That’s why it’s important to have a user journey as well so that the user’s environment, moods, and emotions are kept in mind when the product is being designed. It is human centred design after all.

Sketching

Now that the flows are created, an idea of the product has formed and this is the time where you put pen to paper. Seems simple enough right? But having no previous sketching experience, I was intimidated by the white page and understood the importance of the previous stages in the process. Having solid research and an outline of the features makes the process of sketching much less scary.

Stage 3

Testing

Using Marvel, I was able to bring my paper wireframes to life and make a dynamic prototype to conduct usability tests with. I conducted 5 in person usability tests with friends and adjusted the prototype based on their feedback. This stage was very informative as it was the first time someone else had laid eyes on the project. I enjoyed seeing people’s initial reactions and what grabbed their attention most.

Sketching v2

Based on the participants feedback, I made changes to the home page and the search results. Used colours to indicate to the user where they were in the application. This became the final version of the prototype.

Stage 4

Mockups

8 months and 2 projects later, I wanted to bring the sketches to life by designing them on Figma. I kept the interface design very minimal by changing the font and adding some green color. I used green because it evokes growth and positivity. I felt that some people find that learning words can be stressful. Using a round font and green colour makes the application feel more fun.
