    Gathering Information

    The Challenge

    CareerFoundry's UX Design course focused on applying human centered design thinking principles on a project of our choice. The problem was that there wasn't a resource available for regular people to contact experts.

    For the next 6 months, I iterated through the various steps of design thinking to come up with Guru. I wanted to create a resource to help with life's administrative tasks such as medical appointments, legal services, accounting, etc.

    Competitive Analysis

    I looked at different online platforms that provided this type of service and analyzed what was missing or could be improved upon.

    Existing resources were mostly available to corporations or acted as a business consulting service. Services for personal use had robot messaging systems and dubious results.

    Discovery - Understanding the User

    I conducted user interviews and shared a survey with my personal network. I created affinity diagrams based on those answers.

    I then categorized the answers and split them into three main categories: experts, users, and application.

    This helped me tremendously in making sense of the problem.

    "I wish finding an expert was easier and less intimidating"

    Inclusivity in Design

    Part of my research was also focused on how to make the design more inclusive. During my initial research, I found that the problem was that existing resources were limited to corporations and executives but there wasn't any services available to regular people. I focused on using inclusive language and research methods throughout my project.

    Persona Development

    I created three different personas based on the interview results. I narrowed down the scope of the application so that its objective was to help people connect with experts involved in life's administrative tasks: financing, legal issues, medical, accounting, etc.

    I quickly realized the difficulty that comes with creating a persona free from personal biases and preferences. I was limited to a small interviewee pool and therefore did not have a full picture of the overall population.

    User Flow

    Charlie's goal was to schedule a call with an accountant. I created a task analysis and used that to refine the task's flow. For example, a task would be joining Guru and finding an expert to book a session with.

    Prototyping and Usability Testing

    Testing

    The tests involved six participants located in different parts of the world. This meant that three of the tests were done remotely. I used Google Meet to talk with my participants and asked them to use their mobile and describe to me their action as they went through the various tasks. If you'd like to learn more about the kind of tasks I asked my participants to accomplish, here is a link to my script.

    What I found...

    • Some wished they could see the expert before signing up
    • The design's content was not consistent: clicking on an expert's name brought up another one instead
    • Embrace space

    The tests were a great way to uncover my prototype's shortcomings. Because I had just spent a month learning how to prototype with Adobe Xd, I decided to challenge myself further and get familiar with Figma to create the higher fidelity prototype. Since I had 200 frames to transfer over, I decided to start all over again with a clean slate.

    Attending to the Details

    The Style Guide

    After several iterations of the design, now moved to Figma, I started thinking about the overall interface and what emotions I wanted to convey.

    I also needed a place to lay out all the elements involved in creating Guru to facilitate handoff.

    You can see the full style guide here

    Accessibility

    When creating the style guide, I took a particular interest in using web content usability guidelines as well as following Apple's iOS human interface guidelines to improve Guru's overall accessibility. I paid attention to the contrast levels of the body copy, adding captions to icons, and using inclusive language.

    The Final Prototype

    Guru has gone through many iterations since the start of this design. I expect it will continue to do so as I improve as a UX designer. For now, the available features are signing up, logging in, access to personal profile, searching for an expert, and booking a session (chat, phone, video).

    If time allows, I'd like to create a helpful resources page that is accessible via the Guru logo. I also want to build the chat page so that people have easy access to their messages.
